Nintendo sued the switch host cracking team for more than $150000

Nintendo recently filed a lawsuit against Gary Bowser, the leader of the hacker team team team xecution, for making and selling switch host cracking kits.
Bowser's team produced and sold SX core, SX lite and SX Pro cracking kits to users in batches. Several kits can be used to crack switch hosts at the hardware level. After the player has installed the crack chip on the machine, he can run the self-made system to play games, such as some switch pirated games.
Gary Bowser was arrested by law enforcement last fall, and Nintendo filed a lawsuit against him for making and selling switch host cracking kits. If successful, Bowser will pay $2500 for each cracking kit and $150000 for each copyright violation.
(the previous article amended that it is not a total of US $150000, but a claim of US $2500 for each cracking kit, and a copyright compensation of US $150000 for each cracking kit is attached.)

Intel's latest roadmap has been revealed: the 12-generation Alder Lake-S will be released in the third quarter of this year

The 550Pro exposes Intel's offline PowerPoint presentation, showing that the Alderlake-S, a 12-generation Core for desktop platforms and entry-level workstations, will be released in the third quarter of this year.

According to the leaked roadmap, Intel's line of entry-level workstations equipped with AlderLakes processors will be available by the end of the third quarter of 2021. The roadmap also confirms that processors with 8 large +8 small core configurations will start production as soon as week 35 of 2021, and the 6 large +0 small core version will start production in week 41.

In addition, the leaked roadmap shows that the platform will support the W680 chipset, with processors with TDP ranges from 125W,65W to 35W.
Intel's 12-generation Core Alderlake-S will feature a new, large and small core design, using an enhanced version of the 10nmSuperfin process. Intel will also release a 600-series motherboard with an LGA1700 slot and support for DDR5 memory.
